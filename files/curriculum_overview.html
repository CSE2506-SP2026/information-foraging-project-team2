Curriculum Overview

Our Preschool Curriculum

At Washington University Nursery School, we believe every child is a unique individual with their own pace and pattern of development. Our curriculum is child-centered, play-based, and grounded in Constructivist philosophy. We create meaningful learning experiences that nurture the whole child — socially, emotionally, intellectually, and physically.

Our Educational Philosophy

Guided by the work of theorists such as Piaget, Dewey, and Vygotsky, we believe children learn best through active exploration and meaningful experiences.

  • Structuring classroom experiences around ideas relevant to children’s lives
  • Encouraging hands-on discovery through rich, sensory materials
  • Allowing children to learn at their own pace, independently or collaboratively
  • Asking open-ended questions that promote reasoning and critical thinking
  • Respecting and empowering children as capable problem-solvers
  • Celebrating diversity and honoring similarities and differences within our community

Social & Emotional Development

  • Building self-esteem through communication, responsibility, and participation
  • Encouraging imagination and independent thinking
  • Developing collaboration, compromise, and self-expression
  • Fostering respect for self and others
  • Strengthening problem-solving skills within group settings

Intellectual Development

Language & Literacy

  • Verbally communicating and listening to others
  • Dramatic play and storytelling
  • Singing songs and listening to music
  • Recognizing meaningful words such as names and classroom labels
  • Dictating, illustrating, and writing original stories

Math Exploration

  • Identifying shapes, patterns, and sequences
  • Sorting and categorizing objects
  • Counting and comparing quantities
  • Measuring length and weight
  • Using numbers as symbols to represent quantity
  • Creating simple graphs

Science Discovery

  • Observing seasonal and environmental changes
  • Growing plants and studying life cycles
  • Exploring animals and insects
  • Learning conservation and recycling practices
  • Conducting simple experiments with magnets, heat, and cold
  • Preparing and enjoying healthy snacks

Social Studies & Community

  • Learning about family traditions and cultures
  • Welcoming family visitors to share experiences
  • Exploring the campus and surrounding community
  • Learning about occupations and community roles
  • Engaging in multicultural activities and role-playing
  • Developing empathy and respect for individuals of all abilities

Physical Development

Gross Motor Skills

  • Climbing, sliding, riding trikes, and swinging
  • Throwing, kicking, and bouncing balls
  • Sand play and outdoor exploration
  • Simple games and movement to music
  • Building with large wooden blocks

Fine Motor Skills

  • Tracing, drawing, journaling, and writing
  • Using clipboards indoors and outdoors
  • Creating self-portraits and writing messages
  • Stringing beads and working with manipulatives
  • Building with small objects and collage materials

Visual Arts & Creative Expression

We value creativity as an essential part of learning. Our emphasis is on the process of exploring materials rather than the final product. Children are given time, space, and freedom to experiment and create.

  • Drawing and painting with varied tools and textures
  • Printmaking and stamping
  • Working with clay and dough
  • Cutting, gluing, and constructing
  • Creating collages and 3-dimensional structures
  • Exploring music and dramatic play

Assessment & Family Communication

Teachers observe and assess children’s progress on an ongoing basis. Curriculum experiences are adapted to meet individual needs.

Parent/teacher conferences are held twice a year. The Fall conference is informal and focused on sharing progress and experiences. The Spring conference includes a written assessment covering all areas of development to provide a well-rounded picture of each child.
