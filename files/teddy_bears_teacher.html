Teddy Bear Teachers

Chona Carson

AB Physchology, MAEd

Chona is beginning her 13th year as a teacher in the Nursery School. She teams with Marbie in the Teddy Bear classroom and Marsha in the Panda Bear classroom.

Chona has over 20 years of experience in the early childhood education field. Her degrees include a Bachelor of Arts in psychology from the University of Kansas and a Master of Arts in teaching from Webster University. Chona’s favorite times with the WUNS children include art activities—she enjoys watching them create using different media. Circle time is another favorite of hers, when children enjoy listening to stories and predicting what happens next, as well as singing and counting to teacher-led prompts. For Chona, WUNS is a very special place to teach because of the diverse families that enroll. She also appreciates working with her teammates and collaborating with the other teachers on staff.

Chona is married with two children in college. During her free time, she loves to spend time with her family, read, garden and walk her dogs.

Katie Loynd

BS Elementary Education, MAEd Teaching,
Early Childhood Education

Katie is very excited to begin her fifth year at WUNS, teaching with Chona in the Teddy Bear classroom (mornings) and Colleen in the Sun Bear classroom (afternoons). She has taught kindergarten, first and second grades in the Ladue School District for many years.

She attained her undergraduate degree in elementary education from Creighton University and her Master of Arts in teaching early education from Webster University. Katie is inspired by young children; she is constantly amazed by how they see the joy in simple things. One of her goals as a teacher is to provide an enriching environment that naturally fosters curiosity, engaging conversations, imaginative play, creative movement, and thoughtful expression. She believes WUNS is a special place because of its warm and welcoming energy and its unique location on a college campus.

Katie and her husband Mike are St. Louis natives. They have four children, Alli (21), Jake (19), Elizabeth (17) and Will (14) and a dog Murphy, “the best West Highland White Terrier ever.” In her free time, Katie enjoys hiking, travel, swimming and being with her family.
